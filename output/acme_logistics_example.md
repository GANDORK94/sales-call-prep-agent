## 1. Company Overview

Acme Logistics is a mid-market third-party logistics provider based in the Midwest, serving small and mid-sized shippers across the central US. The company has historically focused on regional freight brokerage and warehousing, and recently expanded into last-mile delivery. They sit in a competitive segment between national 3PLs and regional carriers, where service quality and route density are the main levers of margin.

## 2. Likely Pain Points

**Rising fuel and labor costs are squeezing already thin last-mile margins.**
Last-mile is the most expensive leg of any delivery, and a 3PL new to it has not yet optimized routing density.

**On-time delivery rates are inconsistent across the new last-mile lanes.**
Missed delivery windows trigger chargebacks from shippers and erode retention with the accounts they worked hardest to win.

**Driver utilization is hard to measure without a unified planning view.**
If routing lives in spreadsheets or in the dispatcher's head, capacity decisions are guesses, and the VP has no clean number to defend in QBRs.

**Scaling last-mile means hiring drivers faster than routes can absorb them.**
Without route density, every new driver adds cost faster than revenue.

**Reporting to shippers is manual and reactive instead of proactive.**
A VP of Operations loses hours each week assembling status updates that the system should produce automatically.

## 3. Discovery Questions

1. Walk me through what your team's first 60 days in last-mile looked like. What surprised you, good or bad?
2. How are you measuring route efficiency today, and what does a "good" cost-per-stop number look like for you?
3. When a delivery window is missed, where in the process does the breakdown usually happen?
4. What does your weekly conversation with shippers about service performance sound like right now?
5. If you had a clean view of driver utilization across every lane tomorrow morning, what is the first decision you would make?

## 5. Pre-Call Briefing

- **Anchor the call around:** the last-mile expansion, and whether cost-per-stop is trending in the right direction.
- **Definitely ask:** how route planning happens today, and what the weekly shipper conversation about service quality sounds like.
- **Watch for objection:** "we already have a TMS." Be ready to position around planning quality and driver utilization, not just dispatch.
- **Persona reality:** a VP of Operations is judged on service and cost at the same time. Lead with the cost lever, validate the service angle second.
- **Tone:** peer, not pitch. They are running a real expansion and want a useful conversation, not a deck.
